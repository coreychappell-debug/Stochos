# Global Enterprise Software Accessibility Regulations

When building and selling B2B/Enterprise software like the Stochos Platform into mature global markets, digital accessibility is no longer optional. It is frequently mandated by law or heavily prioritized in government and corporate procurement contracts.

The good news is that **almost all major global markets have harmonized around a single technical standard: WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) 2.1 Level AA.** By building to this standard, you generally ensure compliance across the board.

Here is a breakdown of the specific legal frameworks you must consider across the US, EU, and Greater Asia:

## 1. United States
The US relies on a mix of civil rights laws and strict procurement mandates.

* **Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA):** While originally written for physical spaces, the Department of Justice (DOJ) and US courts strictly enforce the ADA for digital services (Title III for private businesses, Title II for state/local governments). WCAG 2.1 AA is the de facto legal standard. Failure to comply routinely results in high-cost class-action lawsuits.
* **Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act:** This is critical if Stochos intends to sell to the **Federal Government**. Any Information and Communication Technology (ICT) procured by federal agencies *must* be accessible. Your software will often require a Voluntary Product Accessibility Template (VPAT) proving compliance to win these contracts.

## 2. European Union
The EU is currently enacting some of the strictest, most harmonized digital accessibility laws in the world.

* **European Accessibility Act (EAA):** Taking full effect in **June 2025**, this sweeping directive mandates strict digital accessibility for a massive range of private-sector products and services (including e-commerce, banking, and many digital platforms). **Crucially, it applies to any company selling into the EU, regardless of where they are headquartered.**
* **Web Accessibility Directive:** Focuses specifically on public sector websites and mobile apps.
* **The Technical Standard:** The EU relies on **EN 301 549**, which explicitly incorporates WCAG 2.1 AA. 

## 3. Greater Asia
Asia does not have a single harmonized law like the EU, but major markets have enacted robust national anti-discrimination frameworks that mandate WCAG compliance.

* **Japan:** The *Act on the Elimination of Discrimination Against Persons with Disabilities* promotes accessibility. Public sector compliance with national standard **JIS X 8341-3** (which mirrors WCAG 2.1) is mandatory, and it is heavily expected for private corporations doing B2B business.
* **India:** The *Rights of Persons with Disabilities (RPwD) Act* mandates accessibility for both public and private digital services, utilizing standards (GIGW 3.0 / IS 17802) directly aligned with WCAG.
* **South Korea & Singapore:** Both have established digital accessibility standards (KWCAG and IMDA Guidelines, respectively) that reference the WCAG framework.

### Summary Strategy for Stochos
Because the legal landscape relies almost entirely on **WCAG 2.1 Level AA**, you do not need to build different versions of the software for different countries. 

By systematically upgrading the React/Next.js frontend to meet WCAG 2.1 AA (adding semantic HTML, ARIA tags, keyboard navigability, and color contrast), the Stochos Platform will be legally insulated and highly competitive for procurement in the US, EU, and Asian markets.
